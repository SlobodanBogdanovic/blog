In many teams, "QA" and "Testing" are used interchangeably. From an engineering perspective, this is a mistake. Confusing the two leads to bottlenecks and misaligned expectations about who is responsible for quality.

**Testing** is the act of gathering information. Exploring, experimenting, exposing what the system actually does. Testers don't assure quality; they give stakeholders the data to make informed decisions about risk. As Michael Bolton [argued](https://developsense.com/blog/2010/05/testers-get-out-of-the-quality-assurance-business), you cannot test quality into a product after it's built.

**Quality Assurance** is the systemic layer. It covers the processes, standards, and feedback loops that prevent defects from entering the system in the first place — how requirements are written, how code is reviewed, how deployments are controlled.

Testing discovers the current state of the product. QA builds the conditions where a good product is possible.

If you're only testing, you're reacting. If you're practicing QA, you're optimizing.
